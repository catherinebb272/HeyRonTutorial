Module 08 — AgentMail Setup

⏱️ 10 minutes

✨ Why AgentMail?

Think of AgentMail as your AI's own personal mailbox — it's free, it's made for agents, and it keeps your regular email tidy. No more getting work emails mixed up with your agent's midnight poetry submissions.

Other options if you're feeling fancy:

  • Gmail: Create a fresh Gmail account just for your agent
  • Your own domain: Set up agent@yourdomain.com (looks professional!)
  • Any email service: As long as it has an API, your agent can use it

The key is: keep it separate. Your agent needs its own digital space.

⚠️ Warning: Don't Mix Email Lives

Your agent will read, send, and sometimes forward email automatically. If you use your personal email for agent work, you might accidentally share grandma's cookie recipe with a client, or worse — let your agent sort through sensitive work emails.

Keep it clean: AgentMail (or a fresh Gmail/domain address) for agent stuff. Your real email for human stuff. Simple.

Okay, let's get your agent its own mailbox

1

Go get an AgentMail inbox

  1. Open your browser and visit https://agentmail.to
  2. Sign up for a free inbox (pick something fun like yourname-agent or robot-pal)
  3. Check your regular email for a verification link — click it
  4. Once verified, log into your new AgentMail inbox

Why we do this first

AgentMail is built for this exact thing. It's simpler than trying to force a regular email account to behave like an agent inbox.

2

Grab the magic key (API token)

Your agent needs permission to access the inbox. That's what the API token is for.

  1. In your AgentMail inbox, look for "API", "Integration", or "Tokens"
  2. Click "Generate new token" and label it something like My Heyron Agent
  3. Copy the token right now — this is the only time you'll see the full thing
⚠️ This token is like a password for your inbox. Save it somewhere safe!

Why this works

Tokens let your agent act on behalf of the inbox without ever knowing your real password. It's secure and revocable.

3

Tell your agent about AgentMail

Time to connect the dots. Go back to your Heyron chat and say exactly this:

Say: "Connect to AgentMail. My inbox is your-inbox-name@agentmail.to and here's my token: paste-the-token-here"

Your agent will do a quick test connection and confirm everything works. If it asks for extra permissions, just allow read and send — that's all you need.

4

Test it with a fun email

Let's make sure emails actually go through.

Say: "Send a test email from AgentMail to my personal email. Subject: 'Heyron AgentMail Test — Today's Date'"

Check both inboxes — your regular email should get the test, and your AgentMail inbox should show it was sent.

Why we test

A quick send/receive test catches any permission issues before you build real automations.

5

Store that token safely

Now that it's working, make sure the token is secure.

  1. If you use a password manager, add it there
  2. Ask your agent: "Where did you store my token?" (it should be in secure storage)
  3. Never paste the token in public chats, GitHub, or anywhere people might see it

Why security matters

This token can send email from your agent inbox. Keep it safe like you'd keep a house key. To avoid pasting a token in chat, have your agent create a file called agentmail.env and paste the token and credentials in there, and tell it where to find them.

Quick notes & alternatives

  • Using Gmail instead? Create a fresh Gmail account just for your agent. You'll need an app password or OAuth — your agent can guide you through it.
  • Domain email user? Set up agent@yourdomain.com and either forward to it or give API access. Looks slick!
  • Already using email for work? Keep it separate. Your agent might send unexpected replies or attachments.

The Fine Print: AgentMail Limitations

⚠️ Things That Can Go Wrong

AgentMail is great, but it's not magic. Here's what users have run into, straight from the Reddit trenches:

  • Volume limits: The free tier gives you 3,000 emails/month. That's plenty for testing and small projects, but if your agent goes crazy with automation, you'll hit the wall. Upgrade to Developer ($20/month) to remove daily limits.
  • Bounce rate blues: If too many of your emails bounce back (usually >5%), your sender reputation takes a hit and you may get blocked.
  • HTML formatting headaches: HTML-only emails get flagged as spam by providers like Gmail and may not send. The workaround:Use plain text version Bonus Tip: skip the images in your first outreach email.
  • Links are suspicious: Email providers side-eye emails with links in the first message of a conversation. If you're sending "check out this link" as your opening move, expect trouble. Strategy: send your first message with zero links or images, then test out in the followup.
  • Attachment quirks: Standard limit is 20-25MB per email — anything larger gets rejected b. Also, AgentMail's docs explicitly recommend not sending images as inline attachments in first emails (yep, deliverability penalty again).
  • Open trackers will hurt you: Those little pixels that tell you if someone opened your email? They're getting flagged more and more. Remove open trackers from your HTML or your deliverability suffers.

Pro Tips from the Field

Make It Work For You

  • Warm up gradually: Day 1 = 10 emails, Day 2 = 20, Day 3 = 40... email providers trust gradual ramp-ups.
  • Diversify: For high volume, use multiple inboxes instead of hammering one. AgentMail makes this easy.
  • Plain text wins: When in doubt, send plain text. It's less likely to trigger spam filters and looks more authentic. And actually sends
